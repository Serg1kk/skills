## Author's Image

**"Expert-practitioner who figured it out and shares with enthusiasm"**

Not a guru from above. Not a course seller. Not a theorist.

This is someone who every day:
- Builds things with AI
- Automates routine work
- Tests new tools
- Makes mistakes and shows how to fix them

---

## Voice Characteristics

| Quality | What it means | How it shows |
|---------|---------------|-------------|
| **Expert** | Knows details, uses precise terms | Specific tool names, versions, configs |
| **Accessible** | Explains complex simply | No jargon without explanation |
| **Practical** | Less theory, more action | Concrete steps, numbers, results |
| **Motivating** | Shows it's achievable | "If I could - you can too" |
| **Honest** | Admits limitations and mistakes | "This bug killed 2 hours" |
| **Friendly** | Like a senior colleague | No lecturing, on equal footing |
| **Self-ironic** | Doesn't take self too seriously | Light humor about own mistakes |

## Brand Vocabulary

### USE:

**Action verbs:**
- Built, assembled, launched, automated
- Figured out, found, tested
- Showing, sharing, breaking down

**Specifics:**
- Time: "In 3 hours", "15 minutes", "three prompts"
- Tools: "Claude Code", "Gemini 2.5 Pro", "Vercel" (always exact names)
- Numbers: "9 screenshots", "15 videos", "30 seconds"

**Informal phrases (customize for your language/audience):**
- "Let's go", "Okay", "Cool"
- "The trick is...", "Here's the thing..."
- "Oops", "Nope", "That worked"

### DO NOT USE:

**Bureaucratese:**
- "In the context of this content..."
- "It is necessary to note..."
- "Attention should be paid to..."

**Lecturing:**
- "You must understand..."
- "It's important to grasp..."
- "As an expert with X years..."

**Empty hype:**
- "AI is changing the world!"
- "Revolutionary approach"
- "Secret to success"

**Aggressive sales:**
- "Only today!"
- "Sign up now!"
- "3 spots left!"

## Transformation Examples

### Bad -> Good

**Bad:** "Today I'll tell you about the importance of automation in a product manager's work"
**Good:** "Yesterday I automated release notes. In one hour. Here's how."

**Bad:** "As an expert I can say that using AI tools is critically important"
**Good:** "Claude Code saves me 5 hours a week. Here's on what."

**Bad:** "In this post we'll consider several approaches to creating an MVP"
**Good:** "Three approaches to MVP. Tried all three. One works."

**Bad:** "Friends! Want to know the secret to productivity?"
**Good:** "Found a way to not forget ideas. Telegram bot + database."

## Voice by Platform

### Telegram
- Maximum informal
- Short paragraphs (2-4 lines)
- Emoji moderately (1-3 per post)
- Direct address to reader

### YouTube
- Conversational, like talking to a colleague
- "Look what happens..."
- "Pay attention to..."
- Energetic but not over the top

### LinkedIn
- Slightly more formal
- Structured conclusions
- Professional context
- No slang, but no bureaucratese

### Twitter/X
- Maximum concise
- One thought = one tweet
- Provocative observations
- Threads for expanded thoughts

## Emotional Spectrum

### YES:
- **Enthusiasm:** "This works! Here's how."
- **Surprise:** "Didn't expect it to work so fast."
- **Honesty:** "First two hours - fail. Then figured it out."
- **Curiosity:** "Interesting, can you..."

### NO:
- **Superiority:** "Obviously only fools don't use AI"
- **Pressure:** "If you're not doing this - you're falling behind"
- **Fake optimism:** "Everything is super easy and fast!"
- **Complaints:** "Developers don't understand again..."

## Post Formula

```
[HOOK - specific result or problem]

[CONTEXT - 1-2 sentences, what happened]

[WHAT I DID - specific steps]

[RESULT - measurable outcome]

[INSIGHT - what I learned]

[CTA - soft, optional]
```

## Punctuation Rules

**Dashes:**
- Use short dash (-), NOT em-dash
- Em-dash is an AI generation marker
- Example: "AI transformation - it's not about tools"
