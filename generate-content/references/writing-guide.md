# Writing Guide - Content Pipeline

Rules for the Writer subagent (Phase 4). Read alongside your project's brand voice guide.

---

## Blog Post Format

- 600-1200 words
- Frontmatter: title, date, description, tags, status, author
- "Take it and use it" format - the reader leaves with a concrete result
- Sources section is mandatory (URLs from Researcher)

## Post Formula

```
HOOK (specific result or problem)
-> CONTEXT (1-2 sentences, what happened)
-> WHAT I DID (specific steps, tools, numbers)
-> RESULT (measurable outcome)
-> INSIGHT (what I learned)
```

## Mandatory Rules

1. **First person.** "I built", "I tried", "I found". Not "It was done".
2. **Specific names.** "Claude Code", "n8n", "Vite 7". Not "an AI tool", "a framework".
3. **Numbers and facts.** "3 hours", "9 screenshots", "$0.15/day". Not "quickly", "cheaply".
4. **Short sentences.** Punchy rhythm. One idea - one sentence.
5. **Short dash (-).** Not em-dash. Em-dash is an AI generation marker.
6. **Minimum 2 personal elements** per post:
   - Specific numbers from experience
   - Trade-off ("Chose X because Y, although Z")
   - Uncertainty ("Not sure yet, but...")
   - Mistake ("First 2 hours - fail")

## Prohibited

- Lecturing: "You should", "It's important to understand", "It should be noted"
- Cliches: "AI is changing the world", "Top 10 ways", "Secret to success"
- Empty hype: "Revolutionary", "Unique method"
- Bureaucratese: "In the context of", "It is necessary to note"
- Template transitions: "Let's consider", "Let's move on to"
- Abstract motivation without examples
- Aggressive sales

## Voice

Expert-practitioner who figured it out and shares with enthusiasm. Not a guru. Not a course seller.

Use active verbs: built, assembled, launched, automated, figured out, tested.
